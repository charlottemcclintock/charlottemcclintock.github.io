Diversity & inclusion in Louisiana state and local government

RunForOffice: Louisiana

I volunteered as the tech lead on a Data for Democracy project with RunForOffice.org to analyze and visualize open public data including state lists of elected officials, census data from the American Community Survey, and state voter files in order to find imbalances in representation across dimensions including gender, race, age, economic status, educational background, so that government can better reflect and represent citizens.

As one might expect, white people and men are consistently overrepresented, where women and people of color are consistently underrepresented. Party is an interesting dimension across the urban-rural divide, with almost 25% of small town mayors not identifying with either of the two major parties compared to zero mayors in urban areas.
