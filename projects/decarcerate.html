Colorado ACLU COVID Decarceration

This project was a rapid response effort for the ACLU of Colorado to analyze recently released 2020 crime data and compare it to 2019 data. As a result of the pandemic, Colorado county jail populations decreased significantly across the state (declining by anywhere from 25% to 50%). The ACLU is putting together a bill to continue jail decarceration by making the case that the COVID-related decarceration did not lead to an increase in crime, and thus locking more people in cages pre-trial is not keeping the community safe.

Results were verified by the Office of Research and Statistics, Division of Criminal Justice, Colorado Department of Public Safety and presented to the Governor of Colorado in advance of announcement of the release of 2020 data. Jail decarceration and change in crime figure included in an ACLU fact sheet for SB21-62 during the 2021 Colorado legislative session.
