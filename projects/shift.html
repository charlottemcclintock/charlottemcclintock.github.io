Housing affordability and school quality by occupation

Shift Research Lab 2018

During my summer at the Shift Research Lab, I built out an R Shiny web app to explore the effect of occupational structure on housing affordability, school quality, and commute time for the Denver area. The goal of the project is to inform policymakers on the state of housing affordability in Denver in the context of other important factors like school quality, which can act as a proxy for public safety. As this was my first Shiny app, I learned a ton, and really enjoyed working with the reactive contexts available and the freedom and exploration that interactivity allows.

The Housing by Occupational Median Earnings (HOME) Tool integrates occupational data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics, commute data from the Census Bureau, proficiency scores from the Colorado Department of Education, and parcel data from each of the seven counties in the Denver Metro area.
