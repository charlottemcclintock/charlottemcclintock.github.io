Racial disparity in the Charlottesville Child Welfare System

Public Interest Data Lab

I worked as lead researcher and project manager for the UVA Public Interest Data Lab on a two-year project partnering with the Charlottesville Department of Social Services to investigate claims of racial disparity and disproportionality in the Child Welfare System. I started with the Lab in the spring of 2018 as a student in the class, was promoted to lead researcher at the end of the term, and subsequently helped organize and run a continuation of the project with Professor Michele Claibourn, Director of Research & Data Services for the UVA Library.

Children in the foster care system face an incredibly complex set of issues that are often hard to classify. For some children, being reunited with family is a positive outcome. For others, long term placement with a close family friend or family member might be better. More placements means less stability, but leaving a problematic placement could be better than staying. High numbers of face-to-face meetings with a caseworker could be an indicator of frequent movement & behavioral problems, or a caring and attentive caseworker. Academic literature and deep policy readings for context made it possible to investigate claims of disparity while acknowledging uncertainty and complexity inherent in the data.

For more, you can check out the lab website, the open code repositories for the 2018 and 2019 projects, as well as the 2018 report.
