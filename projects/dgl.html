Teaching at the intersection of digital technologies and justice

Digital Governance Lab

In Fall 2019, I co-taught an undergraduate course with a graduate student in Computer Science. The course came about after repeated conversations lamenting the lack of coursework engaging computer science students in critical thought about the disparate impacts of the technologies they are learning to build and contribute to and a similar lack of classes offered which engaged poltics, policy, and social science students on the implications that digital technologies will have on the issues they study. We see a need for a generation of tech literate policymakers and ethically-minded technologists working together to ensure that technologies serve all people more equitably.

We pitched a class, built a curriculum, and taught a small seminar class composed of a diverse group of students from all years and multiple schools and majors spanning technology, economics, politics, and anthropology. We published an open source curriculum on our course website, include a schedule, syllabus, and lesson plans.
