Providing evidence for criminal justice reform in Colorado

Colorado Bail & Incarceration Project

In mid-December, I came across a recently-released dataset from the Vera Institute of Justice with county level incarceration data including both jail & prison data and demographics. I pitched it as a project to Data for Democracy, a global network of data practitioners volunteering on projects for social benefit. I also reached out to some attorneys I know in Colorado and got connected with the research team at the Colorado ACLU.

I am now leading a project analyzing data on money bail for an ACLU report to inform policy and persuade legislators to pass criminal justice reform policies in the upcoming state legsilative session. We have a incredible team of 30 volunteers including working data scientists, undergraduate, masters, and PhD students, and people interested in policy. The project is also a pilot for the Data for Democracy Global Data Ethics Project code of ethics and is a pilot to explore the possibility of ethical assessments at various points in analysis. For more on the project, check out the project repository below and check out D4D if you're interested in joining the team!
