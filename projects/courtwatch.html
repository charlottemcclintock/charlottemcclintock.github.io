Community safety and the carceral state

ACLU of Colorado Court Watch Analysis

As a part of an ongoing relationship with the ACLU of Colorado, I (alongside the wonderful Sam Powers) helped the ACLU of Colorado evaluate a volunteer court watch program and investigate practices in the Denver and Aurora municipal courts.

We examined rates of cash vs. PR bond, presence of counsel, guilty pleas, bond arguments and conditions, demographics of defendants across different courts. Most strikingly (but perhaps unsurprisingly), we found that prosecutors were more likely to ask for cash bond for black defendants, even after controlling for presence of counsel, gender, and charge type. Judges were also more likely to set cash bond for black defendants, but the effect was no longer significant after controlling for prosecutor request.

We are now helping advise the ACLU on their data collection processes for the next iteration of the project, which will be completed virtually during the spring of 2021.
