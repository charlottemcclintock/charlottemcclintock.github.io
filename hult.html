Hult Prize 2018: Boston Regional Final

I competed with a team of graduate and undergraduate students for Hult Prize, a $1 million dollars in seed funding for a scalable, sustainable enterprise designed to impact 10 million lives by 2025. We applied through the general application pool and made it to the Boston Regional Finals. We decided to tackle electricity theft. In developing countries, around a quarter of the electricity produced is stolen from the lines. Theft drives up costs for paying customers, causes black outs, and bankrupts utilities. India currently loses 23% percent of it's generated electricity to theft, costing the companies and the government over 16.2 billion dollars per year.

Elevator Pitch: "Our solution is WaveRider. By disturbing the regular impedance of transmitted electricity, we effectively block electricity theft. WaveRider stops blackouts, increases access, brings new customers into the market, and paves the way for a renewable future. Energy companies will becomes financially sustainable, existing households and businesses will get 24/7 power, and new customers will be connected safely and legally. We target urban divisions with the largest proportion of distribution loss, maximizing our impact."
