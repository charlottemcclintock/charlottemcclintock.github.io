Hi there!

I'm Charlotte.

I’m in my fourth year at the University of Virginia, studying and applying statistics & data science methods to human behavior, politics, and public policy. I just graduated with my BA and am currently completing an accelerated Master’s in Research Methods in Psychology. I'm especially interested in individual choice, collective action, and understanding the variables that influence the critical mass needed for progressive change.

I’m passionate about using data and quantitative insight to inform and improve the work being done by social and political organizations in order to better serve vulnerable populations. A couple recent projects I'm proud of include working with the Thomas Jefferson Area Coalition for the Homeless to understand equity in provision of homelessness services, leading a team of Data for Democracy volunteers on a project for the Colorado ACLU analyzing data from open records requests to inform and direct criminal justice reform proposals during the Colorado legislative session, and helping lead a analysis of administrative foster care data to investigate claims of racial disparity for the Public Interest Data Lab.

I am constantly overwhelmed by the joy of simply being alive. I treasure gratitude, kindness, curiosity, and self-awareness in others and in myself. I love reading books and long-form journalism (especially from Aeon), watching documentaries, sharing ideas, and solving problems. I also love photography, especially when I get the chance to capture joy in action, and I love the clear air in the mountains at home in Colorado.

Thanks for visiting me here! Please reach out if you want to chat!
