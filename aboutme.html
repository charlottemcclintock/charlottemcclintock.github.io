About Me

I'm a second year at the University of Virginia, completing an Echols Interdisciplinary major in Decision Theory & Group Dynamics, a course of study combining behavioral science, politics, economics, statistics, and data science.

Outside of classes, I try to find new ways to learn skills and grow in different ways. I work as a Lead Researcher in the Public Interest Data Lab studying racial disparity and disproportionality in the Charlottesville Child Welfare System. I also work as a research assistant in the Nudge4 Solutions Lab, applying behavioral insights, data science, and interactive technology to develop scalable solutions in education and public policy. I'm also the chair of Assessment & Data Management for the Honor Committee, developing infrastructure for demographic and case data management and organizational assessment to identify problems in the system.
