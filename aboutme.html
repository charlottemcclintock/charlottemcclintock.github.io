About Me

I’m a third year undergraduate student at the University of Virginia studying behavioral science, data science, politics, and economics to understand individual choice and collective action. I'm interested in how radical change happens, finding the threshold between success and failure for social, cultural, and political movements. I want to use patterns and insights derived from empirical quantitative analysis to improve human lives wherever possible, minimizing losses and maximizing collective gain.

I have analyzed confidential foster care data to investigate claims of racial disparity for the Public Interest Data Lab, built an interactive web app to explore occupational structure and housing affordability for the Shift Research Lab, and led a project to analyze diversity, representation, and inclusion in state elected offices with Run for Office. I'm also the chair of Assessment & Data Management for the Honor Committee, developing infrastructure for demographic and case data management and organizational assessment to identify problems in the system. Through my past work, I have become comfortable using R for analysis, but I’m excited by the possibility of learning more frameworks.

I love data analysis & visualization, investigation, and storytelling. I love working through challenging problems to identify a cause or a solution. I work hard. When I love what I do, I run with it as far as I can. I am deeply curious abou the world and love to learn and better myself wherever I can.
