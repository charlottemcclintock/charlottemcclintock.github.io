a short note on an interesting map

distribution of public lands in the US

I grew up in Colorado where we have an absolutely huge amount of public space. The Rocky Mountains are almost all Forest Service land, and public access.

I was looking at spending some time outside near where I'm living now and was puzzled by the lack of big, uninterrupted parks and was curious to find a map with a distribution of public lands in the US.

I wonder what drives this big visual difference? Maybe the ecosystem (much of the southwest is arid desert land) or the Rocky Mountains as a natural barrier? Probably some cultural/political/historical factors?
