Akinyele Omowale Umoja

We Will Shoot Back


this book was such a compelling counterpoint to the histories of the Civil Rights Movement that glorify non-violence as a tactic. Umoja has so many useful ideas about movement building, the utility of national visibility/formal organization (and where that ends), and how southern Black communities coming together to keep each other safe had radical potential and impact.

Umoja tells incredibly detailed stories of the formation of Black paramilitary groups to protect protests, marches, key organizers, and to enforce boycotts. Even while SNCC and the NAACP had national commitments to non-violence, Mississippi organizers had to deal with local realities - armed white supremacist nightriders coming to murder and harm organizers and movement workers in the middle of the night. Umoja makes a compelling case that armed resistance was critical to the organizing of the Mississippi Black Freedom Struggle.

this book has so many interesting stories - how Black haven communities used signals to communicate at night if an unknown car showed up in the neighborhood, a time when local county organizers used the fear of the Black Panthers to scare local white supremacists and stay safe, how bringing white students and northern Black organizers to Mississippi for Freedom Summer played out on the ground, how the Mississippi house passed a bill making organizing boycotts illegal and crippled the local NAACP with financial liabilities for losses to white businesses, and how a couple counties managed boycotts after the ban by avoiding connections to any formal organizations so no one was liable.
